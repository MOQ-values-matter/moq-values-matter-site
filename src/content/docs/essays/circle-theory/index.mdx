## Purpose and Status

Circle Theory (also referred to as Ripple Theory) is an **applied, operational framework** developed to support personal work, coaching, and real-world sense-making.

It is grounded in **Robert M. Pirsig’s Metaphysics of Quality (MOQ)** and is explicitly designed to **operate below Canon level**.

This work:

- ❌ does **not** claim Canon status  
- ❌ does **not** define, formalize, or operationalize Dynamic Quality  
- ❌ does **not** introduce new MOQ levels or laws  

Instead, it seeks to:

- translate MOQ’s descriptive metaphysics into **practical orientation**
- illuminate how value patterns are **lived, carried, reinforced, or degraded**
- provide a **coherent mental model** for navigating real social life

This framework exists as an **Essay-level construction** and should be read as such.

---

## Why This Framework Exists

MOQ offers a powerful metaphysical account of reality as value-driven, structured across physical, biological, social, and intellectual levels. However, MOQ is intentionally **non-operational**.

Circle Theory exists to answer a different class of questions:

- *Where am I actually embedded right now?*
- *Which social circles am I sustaining — or draining?*
- *Why does collapse or renewal rarely stay localized?*
- *Why do certain moral failures (e.g. betrayal) cause disproportionate damage?*
- *Why does meaning feel strong in some contexts and absent in others?*

Rather than proposing new principles, this framework **makes visible** how existing MOQ principles **manifest in lived human systems**.

---

## Core Orientation

At its core, Circle Theory rests on a small set of orienting assumptions:

- **Individuals are value nodes**, not abstractions.
- **Social circles are value fields**, not mere collections of people.
- **Value propagates through action, trust, and sacrifice**, not language alone.
- **Participation creates ripples** that extend across time and across circles.
- **Stability and decay are field phenomena**, not purely individual failures.

The framework treats families, teams, organizations, communities, and nations as **structurally similar value fields**, differing in scale, not in kind.

---

## Relationship to MOQ

Circle Theory is **derivative**, not foundational.

It aligns with MOQ in the following ways:

- Respects the four static value levels (physical, biological, social, intellectual)
- Treats Dynamic Quality as **pre-conceptual novelty**, never as a method
- Maintains clear separation between description (Canon) and application (Essay)

Where MOQ describes **what reality is**, Circle Theory explores **how that reality is inhabited**.

---

## What This Framework Is — and Is Not

### This framework *is*:

- An applied interpretation of MOQ
- A tool for reflection, coaching, and analysis
- Diagram-driven and metaphor-aware
- Explicitly contextual and situational

### This framework is *not*:

- A new metaphysics
- A moral system
- A replacement for MOQ
- A Canon proposal (in its current form)

Any future promotion of ideas from this framework into Canon would require:
- explicit stabilization
- loss of operational specificity
- independent scrutiny

## Intended Use

This framework is intended to be:

- **Used**, not merely understood
- **Questioned**, not believed
- **Refined through practice**, not protected as doctrine

Its value lies not in correctness, but in whether it:
- improves orientation
- clarifies responsibility
- strengthens discernment
- reduces self-deception

If it fails to do so, it should be revised or discarded.

---

## Closing Note

Circle Theory exists in service of **quality of life**, not philosophical completeness.

It is an attempt to take MOQ seriously enough to ask:
> *What does this demand of us, here, now, in the circles we actually inhabit?*

Nothing more — and nothing less.
