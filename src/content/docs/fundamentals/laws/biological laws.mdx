## Scope

These laws describe the **biological resonance level**: living patterns that actively resist entropy through self-maintenance, adaptation, and regeneration.

Biological value is **not mechanical persistence**.
It is *life as dynamic resistance to dissolution*.

## Biological Value in MOQ Terms

> “The premise for an organism’s survival is its ability to relate to reality and to change when its conditions change.”

From this perspective, biological value is governed by **five primary laws**:

1. Preserve pattern  
2. Adapt to changing reality  
3. Reproduce and propagate quality  
4. Cooperate into higher-order systems  
5. Renew through death and regeneration  

These are **meta-laws**, not species-specific behaviors.  
They describe what *must* be true for any biological pattern to persist.

---

## L-B-01: Preserve Pattern (Continuity of Being) {#law-b-01}

**Statement:**  
A biological pattern must preserve itself long enough to remain itself.

**Metaphysical resonance:**
- Life exists as organized resistance to entropy.
- Preservation is not stasis, but maintained coherence under pressure.
- Metabolism, defense, and aggression all serve continuity.

**Quality insight:**  
To live is to hold form against dissolution.

**Observable expressions:**
- Homeostasis  
- Immune response  
- Territorial defense  
- Resource acquisition under scarcity  

---

## L-B-02: Adapt (Dynamic Alignment with Reality) {#law-b-02}

**Statement:**  
Biological patterns must change when conditions change.

**Metaphysical resonance:**
- Static biological patterns persist only by responding to Dynamic Quality.
- Adaptation is the bridge between continuity and change.
- Preservation without adaptation leads to extinction.

**Quality insight:**  
Adaptation is how life preserves quality through transformation.

**Observable expressions:**
- Mutation  
- Learning  
- Behavioral plasticity  
- Environmental specialization  

---

## L-B-03: Reproduce (Propagation of Quality) {#law-b-03}

**Statement:**  
Biological value propagates beyond the individual through reproduction.

**Metaphysical resonance:**
- Reproduction transmits what has proven viable.
- Variation introduces dynamic potential.
- Heredity stabilizes successful patterns.

**Quality insight:**  
Life persists not by copying perfectly, but by copying *well enough*.

**Observable expressions:**
- Cell division  
- Sexual reproduction  
- Genetic inheritance  
- Population continuity  

---

## L-B-04: Cooperate (Integration into Higher Patterns) {#law-b-04}

**Statement:**  
Biological systems increase value by integrating into higher-order structures.

**Metaphysical resonance:**
- Complexity emerges through cooperation, not only competition.
- Each higher level represents a leap in organizational quality.
- Cooperation increases efficiency and systemic stability.

**Quality insight:**  
Higher biological value emerges when parts serve a coherent whole.

**Observable expressions:**
- Multicellularity  
- Symbiosis  
- Social species  
- Ecosystem interdependence  

---

## L-B-05: Renew (Death and Rebirth as Value Dynamics) {#law-b-05}

**Statement:**  
Biological systems must allow parts of themselves to die in order to remain viable.

**Metaphysical resonance:**
- Renewal preserves the integrity of the whole.
- Death is not the opposite of life, but its enabling condition.
- Renewal keeps biological systems open to Dynamic Quality.

**Quality insight:**  
Life stays alive by letting go of what no longer fits.

**Observable expressions:**
- Apoptosis  
- Tissue regeneration  
- Species extinction  
- Ecological turnover

---

## Why There Is No Separate Law for “Expansion” or “Consumption”

Behaviors such as spreading, eating, competing, or dominating are **instrumental**, not fundamental.

They are **context-dependent strategies** serving one or more primary biological laws.

In other conditions:
- contraction
- hibernation
- mutualism
- withdrawal  

may serve the same underlying values more effectively.

Expansion is not a law.  
It is a situational expression of deeper biological value.
