## Purpose of This Cluster

This Canon cluster formalizes the structural relationship between four foundational value patterns:

- **Myth**
- **Integrity**
- **Deception**
- **Truth**

Together, these patterns explain how human systems:
- generate meaning,
- stabilize agency,
- exploit coherence under pressure,
- and correct accumulated error.

This cluster does not introduce new values.  
It **organizes existing Canon entries into a coherent explanatory backbone**.

---

## The Four Patterns (Overview)

### Myth — *Meaning Binding* (Social)
Myth binds individuals into shared identity, obligation, and coordinated action.  
It provides purpose, legitimacy, and justification for sacrifice.

Myth does **not** aim at truth or correction.  
Its function is to mobilize will.

---

### Integrity — *Agency Stabilization* (Social)
Integrity stabilizes social systems by ensuring correspondence between:
- internal commitment,
- expressed intention,
- enacted behavior.

Integrity enables trust, predictability, and long-term cooperation **within myth**, regardless of factual correctness.

Integrity does not require truth.  
It requires consistency.

---

### Deception — *Meaning Exploitation* (Social)
Deception parasitizes myth by intentionally maintaining misalignment between:
- reality constraints, and
- what is presented as necessary or justified.

Deception is socially powerful, especially under stress, but suppresses correction and accumulates hidden failure.

Deception is not error.  
It is intentional asymmetry.

---

### Truth — *Error Correction* (Intellectual)
Truth is the intellectual pattern by which representations track reality closely enough to:
- improve prediction,
- enable correction,
- prevent systemic collapse.

Truth does not bind groups or generate meaning.  
It corrects accumulated distortion.

Truth requires social preconditions—especially integrity—to function at scale.

---

## Structural Roles in the MOQ Hierarchy

| Pattern    | Primary Level | Core Function           |
|------------|---------------|-------------------------|
| Myth       | Social        | Meaning & coordination  |
| Integrity  | Social        | Trust & agency stability|
| Deception  | Social        | Power preservation via distortion |
| Truth      | Intellectual  | Reality correction      |

No pattern in this cluster can be reduced to another without loss of explanatory power.

---

## Healthy System Configuration

A high-quality system tends toward:

- **Myth** that is openly symbolic and revisable  
- **Integrity** that stabilizes roles and commitments  
- **Truth** that corrects error without claiming authority over meaning  
- **Low reliance on Deception**, limited to narrow, temporary contexts  

In such systems:
- meaning binds,
- agency is reliable,
- correction remains possible,
- collapse is avoided or delayed.

---

## Degenerative System Configuration

Low-quality or failing systems exhibit:

- **Myth treated as literal truth**
- **Integrity eroded by incentive misalignment**
- **Deception institutionalized and moralized**
- **Truth suppressed or reframed as disloyalty**

This configuration leads to:
- feedback delay
- accumulated distortion
- sudden legitimacy collapse
- high corrective cost

---

## Dynamic Quality Boundary (Non-Negotiable)

Dynamic Quality (DQ):
- introduces novel reality,
- creates anomalies,
- disrupts static patterns.

Within this cluster:
- **Myth** resists DQ by default
- **Integrity** keeps agents credible under disruption
- **Deception** suppresses DQ by freezing narratives
- **Truth** allows DQ to be recognized and integrated

DQ is not generated or controlled by any pattern here.

---

## Why This Cluster Matters

This quartet explains:
- why societies need meaning but fail without correction
- why integrity can exist without truth
- why deception scales faster than truth
- why truth alone cannot hold a society together

It prevents:
- moralizing social power
- romanticizing truth
- demonizing myth
- psychologizing deception
