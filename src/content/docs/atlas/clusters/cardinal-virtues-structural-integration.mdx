## Purpose of This Cluster

This cluster page formalizes the **structural relationship** between four historically persistent value patterns commonly known as the *cardinal virtues*:

- Temperance  
- Courage  
- Prudence  
- Justice  

Within the MOQ Values Matter framework, these are not treated as moral ideals or personality traits, but as **distinct coordination patterns** that stabilize social systems at scale.

This page introduces **no new claims**.  
It exists to clarify how these patterns **interlock**, **counterbalance**, and **fail** when isolated.

## Structural Overview (Non-Moral)

The four cardinal virtues form a **closed coordination loop** across MOQ levels:

- Two primarily **social restraint / activation patterns**
- One **intellectual calibration pattern**
- One **social integration pattern**

They function together to:
- prevent escalation
- resist coercion
- calibrate action
- legitimate authority

No single virtue is sufficient in isolation.

## Individual Patterns (Structural Roles)

### Temperance — Restraint

**Primary MOQ level:** Social  

Temperance regulates **biologically anchored impulses** through socially internalized restraint.

Structural role:
- Prevents escalation
- Interrupts revenge cycles
- Enables long-horizon cooperation
- Makes loyalty sustainable rather than explosive

Failure mode when isolated:
- Passivity
- Suppression
- Unequal burdening

---

### Courage — Risk-Bearing

**Primary MOQ level:** Social  

Courage enables **action under threat** when avoidance would undermine coordination.

Structural role:
- Resists intimidation and coercion
- Preserves roles under pressure
- Defends norms against erosion

Failure mode when isolated:
- Recklessness
- Domination
- Escalation through force

---

### Prudence — Calibration

**Primary MOQ level:** Intellectual  

Prudence provides **context-sensitive judgment** when rules or impulses are insufficient.

Structural role:
- Calibrates when to restrain or act
- Anticipates downstream consequences
- Prevents rigid rule application

Failure mode when isolated:
- Paralysis
- Technocracy
- Ideological rationalization

---

### Justice — Integration

**Primary MOQ level:** Social  

Justice proportionally aligns **roles, authority, responsibility, and consequence**.

Structural role:
- Binds restraint, risk, and judgment into legitimacy
- Enables cooperation beyond personal trust
- Contains power without constant coercion

Failure mode when isolated:
- Legalism
- Selective enforcement
- Arbitrary authority

---

## Interdependence Logic

The virtues function as **mutual constraints**, not opposites:

- Temperance without Courage → compliance collapse  
- Courage without Temperance → escalation  
- Prudence without Justice → illegitimacy  
- Justice without Prudence → rigid formalism  

Justice depends on:
- Temperance to limit excess
- Courage to defend norms
- Prudence to apply proportion

Prudence depends on:
- Temperance to prevent impulse capture
- Courage to act on judgment

Temperance and Courage depend on:
- Justice for proportional role assignment
